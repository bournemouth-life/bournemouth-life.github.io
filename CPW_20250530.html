Anti-Social Behaviour, Crime & Policing Act 2014
Part 4, Chapter 1, S43 - 58
Community Protection Notice
Written Warning

To: [REDACTED] Date: 30.05.2025
Of: [REDACTED]

Dear, [REDACTED]

Bournemouth, Christchurch & Poole Council are satisfied on reasonable grounds that your behaviour or that of your family or visitors is having a detrimental effect on the quality of life of the local community. The behaviour described below is considered to be unreasonable and persistent in nature.

The purpose of this written warning is to allow you as the responsible person adequate time to stop or remedy the identified behaviour. Failure to rectify the described behaviour will lead to the serving of a Community Protection Notice (CPN). Failure to comply with a CPN is a criminal offence that may result in a fixed penalty notice of £100 or in prosecution that on conviction has a fine of up to £2,500 for individuals and unlimited for businesses and organisations.

The described behaviour is as follows:
BCP ASB Team have reviewed a referral from Dorset Police. Since the 21.03.2025 you have been involved in Three incidents of Anti-Social Behaviour within the BCP Area. Your conduct has and is likely to cause harassment, alarm and distress to members of the public. Your conduct also is capable of causing nuisance or annoyance to members of the public. Within the Referral you have been trespassing at a location your banned from and engaging with members of staff and students that has caused harassment, alarm and distress.

The action you must take to remedy this behaviour to prevent legal action is as follows:
You Must not attend or enter any premises which you have been banned from by formal letter within the BCP Council Area (Bournemouth, Christchurch and Poole). The letter must be issued by someone in authority from those premises, BCP Council Officers or Employees of Dorset Police.

You must take this action within the following timescale:

Immediately upon receipt of this letter

We look forward to your compliance in this matter. If you have any further queries, please contact the issuing officer whose details are below.

This CPN Warning Letter expires six months from the date of issue.
